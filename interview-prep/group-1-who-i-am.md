## Self-Pitch (90 seconds)

> "Tell me about yourself"

My name is Yuri. I'm a Senior DevOps Engineer with over eight years of experience, mostly working with AWS, Terraform, and Kubernetes.

What drives me is not the technology itself — whether it's AI or something more classic. What I care about is the problem behind it. Can this tool actually help the team? How much time does it save? That's the lens I use.

Let me give you two quick examples from my current role.

First: documentation. Our ops engineers were spending two to four hours writing deployment guides for every Terraform module release. I built a tool using Azure OpenAI that generates those guides automatically. Now it takes five to ten minutes. That's about a 95% reduction — and the guides are more consistent than what was done manually.

Second: Terraform plan reviews. Large plans are hard to read, and teams were skimming through them, missing critical changes. I built an AI-assisted tool that summarizes the plan, flags destructive operations, and gives a risk score. Review time dropped by 87%, and junior engineers could suddenly do reviews they couldn't handle before.

My core stack is AWS, Terraform, Kubernetes — I hold a CKA certification — plus GitHub and Jenkins for CI/CD. I've worked across many different domains: healthcare data, MLOps, internal platforms, cloud migrations. I'm used to joining a project, picking it up quickly, and delivering independently.

## Key Strengths

> "What are your key strengths, and how have they helped you in your career?"

Three things stand out.

**First: problem-first thinking.** Before I choose a tool or build something, I ask: what are we actually trying to solve? That keeps me from over-engineering and helps me focus on what the team actually needs.

**Second: I learn fast — and deliberately.** When I needed Kubernetes experience, I didn't just watch videos. I prepared for the CKA certification, failed the first attempt, went back and studied harder, then passed with 94 out of 100. That's how I approach gaps: structured, with a clear goal, not just surface exposure.

**Third: ownership.** On most of my projects, I was the only DevOps engineer. No one to escalate to. So I learned to figure things out, communicate across the whole team, and deliver without much guidance. That independence has been useful in every project I've worked on.
